Reader's Advisory

“Cool Off with a Trip to the North” Book Display

Based on Arctic Dreams by Barry Lopez, this display of nonfiction books about the Arctic is perfect for a summer spent at home. I focused on the following aspects of Arctic Dreams: a setting central to the story, poetic and scientific language, and a variety of characters and stories. Each book would have a short write-up, and the display itself would be decorated with plenty of snowflakes.

  1. A Woman in the Polar Night by Christiane Ritter

    In this 1938 memoir, an Austrian painter recounts her year spent on the island of Spitsbergen in a hut with her husband with wit and awe. Though isolated, endangered, and often underfed, Ritter can’t help but fall under the spell of Arctic wonder.

  2. Names for the Sea by Sarah Moss

    Sarah Moss has dreamed of living in Iceland since summering there as a teenager, so on a whim she applies for a position at the University of Iceland despite her comfortable English life with her family. Amid economic crisis and a volcanic eruption, Moss becomes enchanted with the people and nature of the chilly island, both strange and wonderful.

  3. The Museum of Whales You Will Never See by A. Kendra Greene

    Iceland has a wealth, even a surplus, of museums: 1 for every 10 of its population of 330,000, and A. Kendra Greene takes you on a dreamlike journey through the Arctic past and present as she guides us from the Phallological Museum to the Museum of Sorcery and Witchcraft.

  4. An African in Greenland by Tété-Michel Kpomassie

    It’s more than 4,750 miles from Togo to Greenland, but for Tété-Michel Kpomassie, it was a painless journey to leave his African birthplace in the 1960s to pursue his lifelong Arctic dream. Welcomed warmly by the Inuit, endlessly curious about the country he now called home, Kpomassie introduces the rest of the world to what was only a mystery at the time.

  5. This Cold Heaven by Gretel Ehrlich

    As Gretel Ehrlich tells the story of her travels through Greenland, her obsession for nine years, she also tells the story of the island itself, delving into its rich history and anthropology. Through her eyes, the icy wilderness becomes a fount of wisdom and peace.

  6. True North by Gavin Francis

    In this investigation of Arctic Europe, Gavin Francis covers almost as much ground geographically and he does metaphorically, revealing the present, history, and mythology of every country from the Shetland Islands to Lapland.

  7. The Magnetic North by Sara Wheeler

    Travel to the farthest north one can be with Sara Wheeler as she recounts her voyage spiraling out from the North Pole through the fringes of Russia, the United States, Canada, Denmark, Norway, and Finland, exploring a tapestry of people and cultures bound together in the cold.

  8. The Ice Balloon by Alec Wilkinson

    After explorers like Robert Peary and Frederick Cook fought to become the first person to reach the North Pole, S. A. Andrée decided to float there in a hydrogen balloon, gaining international fame at the time but all but forgotten now. Alec Wilkinson reintroduces us to this remarkable expedition in this well-researched and captivating book.

  9. Saqiyuq by Nancy Wachowich, Apphia Agalakti Awa, Rhoda Kaukjak Katsak, and Sandra Pikujak Katsak

    Named for a strong wind that suddenly shifts direction, this generational memoir follows three women from childhood to raising their own children in the swiftly changing Arctic of the 20th Century. Apphia Awa, born in 1931, lives in a pre-colonial Nunavat; her daughter Rhoda Katsak grows up attending government schools; Sandra Katsak deals with the complexity of a Western settlement.

“A Bright Girl in a Dark World” LibGuide

This LibGuide is built around Sabriel by Garth Nix, a young adult fantasy novel in which the central character must face Death itself and comes out victorious. To make it onto this list, the novels had to feature deft world-building, an intense but hopeful plot, and a main character who is a young woman.

  1. A Great and Terrible Beauty by Libba Bray (Gemma Doyle Trilogy #1)

    A searing look into historical gender roles through the lens of magical realism, this novel follows Gemma Doyle, grieving the loss of her mother, as she begins her first year at a British boarding school in which a small group of girls tap into a power beyond their control.

  2. Enna Burning by Shannon Hale (Books of Bayern #2)

    After becoming friends in The Goose Girl, Isi marries Price Gerric and Enna returns to the forest where she discovers she can wield fire. Convinced she can turn the tide of war, Enna joins her brother and her friends in battle, but can she truly make a difference?

  3. The Kingdom of Back by Marie Lu

    More than anything, Nannerl Mozart wants to be remembered as a great composer, but the world is not ready to accept her, even as her brother Wolfgang achieves fame. When a mysterious stranger offers to make her dreams come true, Nannerl’s eyes are opened to the world of magic in which anything is possible - at a cost.

  4. The Golden Compass by Philip Pullman (His Dark Materials #1)

    Lyra is a young girl who wants to rescue her best friend from the witch clans of the north and gets caught up in her uncle’s plans to build a bridge to a parallel world in this best-selling and much-lauded classic.

  5. The Girl Who Circumnavigated Fairyland in a Ship of Her Own Making by Catherynne Valente (Fairyland #1)

    September is a 12-year-old missing her life in Omaha before the war when she receives a visit from a Green Wind (taking the form of a man) who tells her that Fairyland needs her help. When she decides to join him in his return, she meets the fickle new Marquess of Fairyland, who demands September bring her a talisman from the enchanted woods or face the consequences.

  6. Children of Blood and Bone by Tomi Adeyemi (Legacy of Orisha #1)

    Zélie Adebola remembers life in Orisha before the ruthless king ordered all maji to be killed, when the soil itself hummed with magic, and yearns for it to return. Joining forces with a rogue princess, Zélie seeks to topple the monarchy and prevent magic from being eradicated for good, even as her powers threaten to overwhelm her.

  7. Empire of Sand by Tasha Suri (The Books of Ambha #1)

    When Mehr, the illegitimate daughter of an imperial governor and a nomadic outcast, and her magic, come to the attention of the Emperor, she must evade capture from his terrifying mystics. Will the enslaved gods of her ancestors come to her aid?

  8. Daughter of the Forest by Juliet Marillier (Sevenwaters #1)

    Sorcha mourns the death of her mother, but she and her six brothers find joy in each other. That joy is shattered when an enchantress bewitches her father into marrying her and then curses her brothers – a curse that only Sorcha can lift. Can she complete her quest and save her family, despite being kidnapped by her enemies?
